# Smashed Potatoes

## Ingredients


### Potatoes

- 2 lb small potatoes (fingerling or petite)
- 1 tbsp avocado spray
- Salt and pepper

### Sauce

- 1/2 yellow onion, chopped
- 2 garlic cloves, minced
- 1/4 cup lime juice
- 1 tbsp ginger, shredded
- 1 jalapeño, finely chopped
- 3 tbsp tomato paste
- 2 tbsp soy sauce (or tamari, no additives)
- 1/2 cup coconut milk (from can, no additives)
- 1/2 cup greek yogurt (nonfat)
- 1 tsp olive oil (or avocado oil)
- Optional toppings:
- Green onion, chopped
- Red pepper, finely diced

## Instructions

1. Preheat the oven to 400°F.
2. Boil the potatoes for about 30 minutes, or until tender.
3. Drain and let them dry for 5 minutes.
4. Transfer to a parchment-lined baking sheet and smash flat using a
fork or the palm of your hand. The thinner they are, the crispier they’ll
get.
5. Spray lightly with avocado oil and season with salt and pepper.
6. Bake for 40–45 minutes, until golden and crisp.
Make the Sauce:
7. While the potatoes bake, sauté the chopped onion and minced garlic
in 1 tsp olive oil or avocado spray over medium heat until translucent.
8. Deglaze with 1/4 cup lime juice, scraping up any bits from the pan.
9. Add the chopped jalapeño and shredded ginger, and
cook for 1–2 minutes.
10. Stir in the tomato paste and cook another 1–2 minutes.
11. Cut the heat and stir in the soy sauce.
12. As the sauce cools slightly, mix the coconut milk and
greek yogurt together in a separate bowl, then slowly add
that mixture to the pan. Stir until the sauce becomes smooth
and orange.
13. Once the potatoes are done, toss them with the sauce
until evenly coated.
14. Top with chopped green onion and red pepper if
desired.
