# Carrot Cake Cookies

## Ingredients


### Dry

- 1 1/2 cup oat flour
- 2 tsp cinnamon
- 1/2 tsp baking soda
- 1/4 tsp baking powder
- 1/4 tsp salt

### Wet

- 1 1/2 cup carrots (shredded)
- 3 eggs
- 1 tsp pure vanilla extract
- 1/4 cup honey
- 1/2 cup greek yogurt (nonfat)

### Frosting

- 2 cup greek yogurt (nonfat)
- 2 scoops vanilla protein powder
- 3 tbsp chia seeds
- 1/2 tsp cinnamon
- Pinch of salt
- optional: 1/2 tsp pure vanilla extract

### Topping

- 9 walnuts (≈20g)

## Instructions

1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
2. Make the frosting:
In a small bowl, mix together greek yogurt, vanilla
protein powder, chia seeds, cinnamon, salt, and
vanilla extract (if using). Place in the fridge while you
make the cookies. For a version without protein
powder, substitute a drizzle of honey for sweetness
— just note it will need extra time in the fridge to
thicken.
3. Mix the dry ingredients:
In a large bowl, whisk together oat flour, cinnamon,
baking soda, baking powder, and salt until well
combined.
4. Add wet ingredients:
Stir in shredded carrots, eggs, vanilla, honey, and greek
yogurt. Mix until smooth and combined.
5. Chill:
Place the dough in the fridge for 15–20 minutes to firm up.
6. Bake:
Use an ice cream scoop or large spoon to portion the dough
into 9 even rounds on a parchment-lined baking sheet. Bake
for 10–12 minutes, or until lightly golden on top — don’t
overbake; pull out while still soft.
7. Cool and finish:
Let cookies cool completely before frosting. Top each
cookie with a walnut, then store in the fridge after serving.
