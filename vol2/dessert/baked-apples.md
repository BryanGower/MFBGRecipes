# Baked Apples

## Ingredients


### Apples

- 1 large apple (Honeycrisp)
- 1 tsp honey (drizzled over each half before
- baking)
- Pinch of salt
- 1/4–1/2 cup water (for baking dish)

### Oat Mixture

- 1/3 cup old-fashioned oats
- 2 tsp honey
- 1/2 tsp pure vanilla extract
- 1/2 tsp cinnamon
- 5 walnuts (≈12g), finely chopped
- Pinch of salt

### Date Caramel

- 2 dates (≈25g)
- Small splash of water or milk to soften and mash)
- Pinch of salt

### Optional Topping

- A scoop of greek yogurt (full-fat)

## Instructions

1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
2. Slice the apple in half lengthwise. Use a spoon to
remove the core and some of the center flesh,
creating a hollow center.
3. Sprinkle each apple half with a pinch of salt and
1/2 tsp of honey drizzled over each.
4. Place apples cut-side up in a small dish. Set it
inside a larger pan with about 1/2 inch of water
(around 1 cup) to steam and soften. Bake for 25
minutes.
5. While apples bake, prepare the oat mixture:
combine oats, 2 tsp honey, vanilla, cinnamon,
chopped nuts, and salt until slightly sticky and moist.
6. Remove apples from oven, spoon the oat mixture
into the hollowed centers, and return to oven. Bake for
another 15 minutes, until golden and softened.
7. While the apples finish baking, make the date
caramel: soak dates in hot water or warm milk for a
few minutes. Mash with a fork, adding a splash of
liquid and a pinch of salt, until smooth and caramellike.
8. Serve apples warm, topped with the date caramel
and a scoop of Greek yogurt if desired.
