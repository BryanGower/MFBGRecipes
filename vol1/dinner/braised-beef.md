# Braised Beef

## Ingredients

- 48 oz (3lbs) beef chuck
- 3 cloves garlic (minced)
- 1 cup mushrooms
- 2 large carrots (chopped)
- 1 onion (diced)
- 4 cups beef stock or bone broth (plus extra as needed)
- 2 tbsp olive oil or avocado oil
- Optional:
- Potatoes

### Seasonings

- 1 tbsp fresh rosemary
- 2 tbsp italian seasoning
- 2 tsp paprika
- Salt & pepper (to taste, plus more as needed)

## Instructions

INSTRUCTIONS BRAISED BEEF 1. Preheat Oven: Set your oven to 375°F (190°C).
2. Prepare the Vegetables: Chop the onions, carrots, and
garlic.
3. Trim and Season the Beef:
If the beef chuck is very fatty, trim off excess fat, but leave
some for flavor.
Pat the beef dry with paper towels.
Generously season it on all sides with salt and pepper.
4. Sear the Beef:
Heat 1 tbsp of olive oil or avocado oil in an oven-safe pan
(Dutch oven is ideal) over high heat.
Sear the beef for 3–4 minutes per side until deeply
browned.
Once seared, remove the beef and set it aside.
5. Sauté the Aromatics:
Lower the heat to medium.
Add the garlic, onion, and carrots to the pan and cook until
soft and fragrant.
6. Deglaze the Pan:
Pour in beef stock or bone broth.
Use a wooden spoon to scrape up the browned bits from
the pan (this is where the flavor is).
7. Assemble & Braise:
Turn off the heat and place the seared beef back in the
pan.
Add mushrooms, rosemary, Italian seasoning, paprika, extra
salt and pepper—plus more carrots or other veggies if
you’d like.
The broth should come just below the top of the beef, with
the meat slightly peeking above the liquid.
8. Cook in the Oven:
Cover the pan with a lid and place it in the oven.
Braise for 3–4 hours, checking every 45 minutes.
If the liquid level gets low, add more broth.
Taste and adjust seasonings as needed throughout the
cooking process.
The beef is ready when it’s fork-tender and easily pulls
apart.
